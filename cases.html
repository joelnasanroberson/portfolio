Case Study 01 — Premium Launch: London Market for Actors

Summary

Inside a members-only studio with a modest budget, I launched the company’s highest-priced program—an in-person UK networking track for American actors—resulting in 90 enrollments and $247,500 in revenue, plus a waitlist. Budget used: $100 for a targeted IG validation pilot and $200/month in SMS credits.

Context & Goal

Organization: One on One / Next Level (members-only community)

Market reality: Some UK agencies had been skeptical of American talent; credibility and proof mattered more than volume.

Objective: Position a premium, scarce-seat offer to members, fill the cohort, and validate a repeatable, low-waste launch model.

Constraints

  • Audience: Closed—members only (no broad top-of-funnel buying).
  • Budget: $100 IG pilot to validate creative; $200/month SMS credits (strict pacing to avoid overage).
  • Timing: Limited application window with specific talent/partner availability.

Strategy

  • Positioning: Lead with credibility—“Serious access, real rooms.” Back the promise with partner Q&As, faculty reels, and alumni outcomes.
  • Lifecycle engine (owned channels first): Sequenced email drips and segmented SMS (credit-aware), plus partner co-marketing, targeted phone outreach, and in-studio print moments.
  • Clear, proof-forward landing page: Concise copy, real quotes/logos/clips, and a short application path.
  • Team enablement: Staff training and talk tracks to confidently sell in-room during programming.
  • Focused experimentation: A/B test message angles (“Access to reps” vs. “UK booking pipeline”), micro-CTA language, and SMS timing to maintain urgency without fatigue.

Execution Notes

  • Built a full-stack GTM around owned media; the IG spend was intentionally tiny and used primarily for creative validation (thumb-stop rate, angle testing).
  • Produced partner interview prompts; cut short video and quote assets to anchor emails and landing copy.
  • Implemented SMS segmentation, throttling, and time-of-day delivery to stay within $200/month credits while preserving response quality.

Outcomes

  • 90 seats sold at an average $2,750 per seat → $247,500 in revenue.
  • Program became the top performer of the past two years; waitlist created for the next cohort.
  • Messaging costs were contained; member experience remained high (no over-messaging, no public “hard sell”).

Why It Worked

  • We matched credible proof to an audience that already trusted the brand (members).
  • The funnel emphasized clarity and respect: succinct value, real outcomes, and minimal friction.

What I’d Scale Next

  • Add retargeting (Meta/YouTube) around the winning landing page and creative.
  • Launch a member referral mini-program (faculty clip + limited perk) and a 3-email win-back for waitlisted members.
  • Test a non-member RSVP gate (information session) that still routes through a membership-first path.

Capabilities Used
Email/SMS automation • CRM segmentation • Light paid social • Copy & landing page testing • Video/motion/photography • Team enablement

Case Study 02 — Search Engine Growth: SEO + Google Ads Overhaul

Summary

I introduced modern SEO/SEM and Google Ads from zero, established measurement, and built a search operating system that the team can sustain. Over the full period analyzed, results included ~655,280 impressions, ~23,000 clicks (CTR ~3.5%), $9,900 in spend (CPC ~$0.43), and 3,453 membership signups tracked as conversions. In Aug ’25, CTR rose to ~5.7% (CPC ~$0.61), indicating healthy demand with room to tighten query efficiency.

Definition: In this program, “conversions” = membership signups. Member spend ranges from $1,000–$50,000. With an average cost per signup ≈ $2.87 ($9,900/3,453), acquisition efficiency was exceptionally strong; realized ROI depends on the final mix of member spend within that range.

Context & Goal

  • Starting point: No contemporary SEO/SEM practice; no active Google Ads; limited tagging.
  • Objective: Make the brand consistently visible on search, capture intent efficiently, and create a measurement spine the org can trust.

Approach

  • Measurement foundation: GA4, a clean UTM taxonomy, and clear conversion definitions; Google Search Console for query/index health; weekly dashboards and monthly readouts.
  • SEO fundamentals: Keyword map → priority on-page fixes → internal linking; branded search improved to average #2.
  • Google Ads from scratch: Intent-based campaign/ad-group structure, disciplined match types, a negative keyword library, remarketing audiences, and ad extensions/sitelinks.
  • Creative ↔ landing loop: Headlines aligned to intent, proof above the fold, and reduced form friction; iterative testing to keep only what wins.

Implementation Highlights

  • Ongoing Search Terms hygiene to cut waste and protect CPC.
  • Audience layering (remarketing; compliant email seeds), geo/device adjustments where performance diverged.
  • Copy frameworks tailored to intent (e.g., “Book more auditions,” “Level up your reps,” “From NYC to London”) with matching page sections.

Outcomes

  • All-time: ~655k impressions • ~23k clicks (CTR ~3.5%) • $9.9k spend (CPC ~$0.43) • 3,453 membership signups (tracked conversions).
  • Recent month (Aug ’25): 20,675 impressions • 1,180 clicks • CTR ~5.7% • $723.43 spend • CPC ~$0.61.
  • Durable assets left behind: tagging standards, negative-keyword hygiene, Search Console cadence, audience segments, and a test-and-learn roadmap.

Commercial Lens

At ~$2.87 per signup, acquisition efficiency was unusually strong; priority is keeping lead quality and attribution clarity tight so performance reflects true revenue, not micro-events.

What I’d Do Next

  • Tighten query quality: Expand negatives; pin exact on proven terms; rebalance phrase/broad.
  • Bid strategy: After traffic cleanup, test tCPA or Max Conversions with guardrails.
  • Conversion hygiene: Elevate primary conversions (signups); move micro-events to secondary tracking where relevant.
  • Landing pages: Continue headline and proof-block tests; monitor form friction and time-to-apply.

Capabilities Used
Google Ads • GA4 • Google Search Console • UTM tagging • Audience/remarketing • Landing page testing • Intent-based copy frameworks
