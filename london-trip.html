Case Study 01

Premium Launch: London Market for Actors

Inside a members‑only studio and modest budget, I launched the company’s highest‑priced program—an in‑person UK networking track for American actors—resulting in 90 enrollments and $247,500 revenue, plus a waitlist. Budget used: $100 for a targeted IG validation pilot and $200/month in SMS credits.

Context & Goal

Organization: One on One / Next Level (members‑only community)

Market reality: Some UK agencies had been skeptical of American talent; credibility and proof mattered more than volume.

Objective: Position a premium, scarce‑seat offer to members, fill the cohort, and validate a repeatable, low‑waste launch model.

Constraints

  • Audience: Closed—members only (no broad top‑of‑funnel buying).
  • Budget: $100 IG pilot to validate creative; $200/month SMS credits (strict pacing to avoid overage).
  • Timing: Limited application window with specific talent/partner availability.

Strategy

  • Positioning: Lead with credibility—“Serious access, real rooms.” Back the promise with partner Q&As, faculty reels, and alumni outcomes.
  • Lifecycle engine: Sequenced email drips and segmented SMS (credit‑aware), plus partner co‑marketing, targeted phone outreach, and in‑studio print moments.
  • Landing page: Concise copy, real quotes/logos/clips, and a short application path.
  • Team enablement: Staff training and talk tracks to confidently sell in‑room during programming.
  • Focused experimentation: A/B test message angles ("Access to reps" vs. "UK booking pipeline"), micro‑CTA language, and SMS timing to maintain urgency without fatigue.

Execution Notes

  • Built a full‑stack GTM around owned media; the IG spend was intentionally tiny and used primarily for creative validation (thumb‑stop rate, angle testing).
  • Produced partner interview prompts; cut short video and quote assets to anchor emails and landing copy.
  • Implemented SMS segmentation, throttling, and time‑of‑day delivery to stay within $200/month credits while preserving response quality.

Outcomes

  • 90 seats sold at an average $2,750 per seat → $247,500 in revenue.
  • Program became the top performer of the past two years; waitlist created for the next cohort.
  • Messaging costs were contained; member experience remained high (no over‑messaging, no public “hard sell”).

Why It Worked

  • We matched credible proof to an audience that already trusted the brand (members).
  • The funnel emphasized clarity and respect: succinct value, real outcomes, and minimal friction.

What I’d Scale Next

  • Add retargeting (Meta/YouTube) around the winning landing page and creative.
  • Launch a member referral mini‑program (faculty clip + limited perk) and a 3‑email win‑back for waitlisted members.
  • Test a non‑member RSVP gate (information session) that still routes through a membership‑first path.
