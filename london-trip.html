Case Study 01

Premium Launch: London Market for Actors

Inside a members‑only studio with a modest budget, I launched the company’s highest‑priced program — an in‑person UK networking track for American actors — resulting in 90 enrollments and $247,500 in revenue, plus a waitlist. Budget used: $100 IG creative validation pilot and $200/month in SMS credits.

Members‑only Owned‑channel GTM Proof‑forward landing Email + segmented SMS

Context & Goal

Organization: One on One / Next Level (members‑only community)

Market reality: Some UK agencies had been skeptical of American talent; credibility and proof mattered more than volume.

Objective: Position a premium, scarce‑seat offer to members, fill the cohort, and validate a repeatable, low‑waste launch model.

Constraints

  • Audience: Closed — members only (no broad top‑of‑funnel buying).
  • Budget: $100 IG pilot to validate creative; $200/month SMS credits (strict pacing to avoid overage).
  • Timing: Limited application window with specific talent/partner availability.

Strategy

  • Positioning: Lead with credibility — “Serious access, real rooms.” Back with partner Q&As, faculty reels, and alumni outcomes.
  • Lifecycle engine: Sequenced email drips and segmented SMS (credit‑aware), plus partner co‑marketing, targeted phone outreach, and in‑studio print moments.
  • Clear landing page: Concise copy, real quotes/logos/clips, and a short application path.
  • Team enablement: Staff training and talk tracks to confidently sell in‑room during programming.
  • Focused experimentation: A/B test angle (“Access to reps” vs. “UK booking pipeline”), micro‑CTA language, and SMS timing to maintain urgency without fatigue.

Execution Notes

  • Owned‑media GTM; IG spend used mainly for creative validation (thumb‑stop rate, angle tests).
  • Partner interview prompts → short video + quotes for emails and landing copy.
  • SMS segmentation, throttling, and time‑of‑day delivery to fit $200/month credits while preserving response quality.

Outcomes

  • 90 seats sold at an average $2,750 per seat → $247,500 revenue.
  • Program became the top performer of the past two years; waitlist created for the next cohort.
  • Messaging costs contained; member experience remained high (no over‑messaging, no public “hard sell”).

Why It Worked

  • Matched credible proof to an audience that already trusted the brand (members).
  • A funnel emphasizing clarity and respect: succinct value, real outcomes, minimal friction.

What I’d Scale Next

  • Add retargeting (Meta/YouTube) around the winning landing page and creative.
  • Launch a member referral mini‑program (faculty clip + limited perk) and a 3‑email win‑back for waitlisted members.
  • Test a non‑member RSVP gate (information session) that still routes through a membership‑first path.

Capabilities — Email/SMS automation • CRM segmentation • Light paid social • Copy & landing testing • Video/motion/photography • Team enablement
