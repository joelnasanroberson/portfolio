Case Study 02

Search Engine Growth: SEO + Google Ads Overhaul

Introduced modern SEO/SEM and Google Ads from zero, put measurement in place, and built a sustainable search operating system. Results across the analyzed period: ~655,280 impressions, ~23,000 clicks (CTR ~3.5%), $9,900 spend (CPC ~$0.43), and 3,453 membership signups. In Aug ’25, CTR rose to ~5.7% (CPC ~$0.61).

Context & Goal

  • Starting point: No contemporary SEO/SEM practice; no active Google Ads; limited tagging.
  • Objective: Make the brand consistently visible on search, capture intent efficiently, and create a measurement spine the org can trust.

Approach

  • Measurement foundation: GA4, a clean UTM taxonomy, clear conversion definitions; Google Search Console for query/index health; weekly dashboards and monthly readouts.
  • SEO fundamentals: Keyword map → priority on‑page fixes → internal linking; branded search improved to average #2.
  • Google Ads from scratch: Intent‑based campaign/ad‑group structure, disciplined match types, a negative keyword library, remarketing audiences, and ad extensions/sitelinks.
  • Creative ↔ landing loop: Headlines aligned to intent, proof above the fold, and reduced form friction; iterative testing to keep only what wins.

Implementation Highlights

  • Ongoing Search Terms hygiene to cut waste and protect CPC.
  • Audience layering (remarketing; compliant email seeds), geo/device adjustments where performance diverged.
  • Copy frameworks tailored to intent (e.g., “Book more auditions,” “Level up your reps,” “From NYC to London”) with matching page sections.

Outcomes

  • All‑time: ~655k impressions • ~23k clicks (CTR ~3.5%) • $9.9k spend (CPC ~$0.43) • 3,453 membership signups (tracked conversions).
  • Aug ’25: 20,675 impressions • 1,180 clicks (CTR ~5.7%) • $723.43 spend (CPC ~$0.61).
  • Durable assets left behind: tagging standards, negative‑keyword hygiene, Search Console cadence, audience segments, and a test‑and‑learn roadmap.

Commercial Lens

“Conversions” = membership signups. Member spend ranges from $1,000–$50,000. With an average cost per signup ≈ $2.87 ($9,900/3,453), acquisition efficiency was unusually strong; realized ROI depends on the final mix of member spend within that range.

What I’d Do Next

  • Tighten query quality: expand negatives; pin exact on proven terms; rebalance phrase/broad.
  • Bid strategy: after traffic cleanup, test tCPA or Max Conversions with guardrails.
  • Conversion hygiene: elevate primary conversions (signups); move micro‑events to secondary tracking where relevant.
  • Landing pages: continue headline and proof‑block tests; monitor form friction and time‑to‑apply.
